# Offer Thesis

Broad consulting was rejected because it weakens credibility at the exact stage where this practice needs clarity most. A solo consultant trying to win early work is more believable when the niche, buyer problem, and delivery scope are tightly defined.

Learning and programme operations is the strongest springboard because it matches the founder's strengths in KPI design, reporting clarity, workflow logic, and management-facing analytics. It also addresses a real gap: many organisations already have systems for administration, but not a reliable layer for decision support.

This niche creates a practical wedge. It is narrow enough to differentiate, broad enough to contain multiple client types, and commercially sensible for a solo operator. Once proof, referrals, and recurring work are established, the practice can expand carefully from this base rather than starting broad and sounding generic.
